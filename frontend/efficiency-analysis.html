Why Efficiency Analysis?

Raw energy consumption data is misleading when buildings vary significantly in size. A 100,000 sq ft science building naturally uses more energy than a 10,000 sq ft office. Energy efficiency metrics normalize consumption by building size, enabling fair comparisons and identifying true inefficiencies.

üéØ Core Principle

Energy Intensity (EUI)
Energy per Square Meter = Total Usage / Gross Area

This metric creates a level playing field for comparing buildings regardless of their physical size.

üìä Analysis Approach

  • Normalize energy by square footage
  • Establish campus-wide baseline
  • Flag buildings exceeding baseline
  • Apply Pareto analysis (80/20 rule)

üí° Business Impact

Identify the 20% of buildings responsible for 80% of excess energy, enabling focused interventions with maximum return on investment.
